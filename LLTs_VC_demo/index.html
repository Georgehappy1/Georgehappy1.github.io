LLTs: non-parallel many-to-many voice conversion using local linguistic tokens

Authors: Chao Wang, Yibiao Yu

Abstract: Autoencoder based voice conversion model has lately received significant attention in non-parallel many to many voice conversion, where the encoder learns to disentangle the linguistic content from the speaker identity and the decoder learns to generate the target utterance by conditioning the encoder output on the target speaker representation. Despite the enormous progress in this area, it is still challenging for the encoder to find a proper balance between removing the speaker information and preserving the linguistic content. To address this issue, we propose local linguistic tokens(LLTs), a set of speaker-invariant phonetic embeddings jointly trained with the auto-encoder model using multi-head attention. Both objective and subjective experimental results revealed that, compared with the state-of-the-art VQ-VAE method, the proposed LLTs method achieved significantly higher speech quality and comparable speaker similarity.
